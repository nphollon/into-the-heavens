Projections

WebGL by default shows an orthographic projection.

For first-person games, orthographic projections don't cut it. In an orthographic projection, things remain the same size as they move farther away from the camera. So for programs where a perspective view is needed, we use what cartographers call a gnomonic projection. The gnomonic projection is unique in that it preserves straight lines. This gives a view of the world that looks realistic, but unfortunately it is only useful for narrow fields of view.

For fields of view larger than an octant, stereographic projections are much better than gnomonic projections. An entire hemisphere can be shown with bearable distortion. Unfortunately, straight lines in space no longer look straight to the camera.

If you have a regular (gnomonic) projection matrix computed, you can easily use it to create a stereographic projection. Before multiplying the vertex position by the projection matrix, push the vertices away from the camera.

position.z = position.z + length(position)
